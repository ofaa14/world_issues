Environmental Problems

Climate Change

What is it?

Climate Change is any long-term change in the expected patterns of average weather of a region over a significant period of time. Some examples include; changes in solar output and volcanism. Several human activities are presently driving driving climate change through global warming.

Deforestation

What is it?

Deforestation is the process of forests being destroyed with the requirements of cutting down trees and not replanting them. This often occurs when people turn forests into farms, ranches, cities, etc. Its most common cause obtains wood and fuel.

Pollution

What is it?

Pollution is the addition of any substance or form of energy to the environment at a rate faster than it can be dispersed or stored in a harmless form. It is mostly caused by the burning of fossil fuels, such as coal, oil, natural gas and gasoline (which is used to produce electricity and power vehicles).

Social Issues

Gender Education Disparity

What is it?

Gender education disparity is the imbalance between sexes regarding enrollment, dropout rates, and survival rates pertaining to education. Predominantly in developing countries, gender disparities in education occur when one gender is disadvantaged over the other in terms of schooling. In these areas, females enrolled in schools and the workforce are significantly lower while the percentage of males recieving formal education remains high.

Residential Segregation

What is it?

Segregation in neighborhoods based on race, ethnicity, income, etc. While overt discrimination is outlawed, segregation based on race, income, and socioeconomic status is still present in many neighborhoods. These trends can be attributed to suburbanization, discrimination, and personal preferences.

Poverty

What is it?

Poverty is defined as living without the basic necessities of life: food, clothing, and housing. It is a multifaceted concept, which may include social, economic, and political elements. The unequal distrubution of wealth in countries is the most pronounced, it's been leading to the poverty we see today. These people in 1st world countries tend to work minimum wage jobs. Those in third world countries tend to work in sweatshops for little pay.

Violence Against Women

What is it?

Violence against women is a global pandemic that comes in many forms. The forms of violence include: physical, sexual, and psychological (both in public and private spaces). One out of three women deal with the troubles of being physically or sexually abused by an intimate partner.

LGBTQ+ Discrimination

What is it?

LGBTQ+ discrimination is a predisposition towards heterosexual people, which is biased against lesbian, gay, bisexual, transgender, and queer people, among others. Many LGBTQ+ individuals often feel alone due to the outrageous and unrighteous acts which discriminate against them. {LGBTQ+ Stattistics: 85% have experienced verbal harassment; 58% have felt unsafe at school due to their sexual orientation; 43% have felt unsafe because of their gender identity; 27% have been physically harassed at school because of their sexual orientation; 13% have been physically harassed because of their gender identity.}
